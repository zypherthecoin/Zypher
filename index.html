The Problem

American healthcare loses billions
to a broken data layer.

Provider directories are inaccurate. Billing systems are fragmented. No unified, real-time source of provider truth exists — and hospitals pay for it every year.

$80B+
Uncompensated Care Annually
U.S. hospitals absorb over $80 billion per year in uncompensated care — services rendered to patients whose claims fail due to data errors, eligibility failures, or billing fragmentation.
~50%
Provider Directory Error Rate
Studies show provider directory error rates as high as 50% in certain markets. Outdated records cause claims denials that cannot be corrected retroactively, costing providers billions.
0
Real-Time National Provider Registry
The federal NPPES registry is batch-updated and siloed. No shared, real-time, composable infrastructure exists to synchronize provider data across payers, clearinghouses, and health systems.
The Solution

On-chain provider infrastructure,
anchored to federal data.

Zypher ingests authoritative federal data and anchors it permanently on Solana — creating a composable, verifiable provider registry any participant can query.

1
Federal Data Ingestion
NPPES, CMS, and HRSA data pipelines are connected and continuously ingested into the Zypher network.
2
Verification & Hashing
Provider records are normalized, validated, and cryptographically hashed using SHA-256.
3
On-Chain Anchoring
Verified hashes are anchored to Solana, creating an immutable audit trail linked to each provider NPI.
4
Composable Access
Any participant — payer, clearinghouse, or patient — can query verified provider data in real time via ZPH.

New to Crypto?

How to buy ZPH —
step by step.

Never bought crypto before? No problem. Here's everything you need to go from zero to holding ZPH in under 15 minutes.

1
Download a Solana wallet
ZPH lives on the Solana blockchain, so you'll need a Solana-compatible wallet. Phantom is the most popular and beginner-friendly option — it works as a browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Brave) and as a mobile app (iOS & Android).
Download Phantom at phantom.app — always verify you're on the official site before installing.
2
Create your wallet & save your seed phrase
When you set up Phantom, it will generate a seed phrase — 12 random words that are the master key to your wallet. Write these down on paper and store them somewhere safe offline. Never share your seed phrase with anyone, ever. Anyone who has it can access your funds.
Zypher will never ask for your seed phrase or private key. Any message asking for these is a scam.
3
Fund your wallet with SOL
To buy ZPH, you first need SOL — Solana's native currency — in your wallet. You can buy SOL from major exchanges like Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance using a bank transfer or debit card, then send it to your Phantom wallet address. You'll need a small amount of SOL (even $10–$20 worth) to get started, plus a tiny bit extra to cover transaction fees (usually less than $0.01).
Your Phantom wallet address starts with a string of letters and numbers. Copy it from the top of the Phantom app to receive SOL.
4
Go to Jupiter and connect your wallet
Jupiter (jup.ag) is Solana's leading token swap platform — it finds the best price across all DEXes automatically. Go to Jupiter, click Connect Wallet in the top right, and select Phantom. Approve the connection in the Phantom popup.
Connecting your wallet to Jupiter does not give it permission to spend your funds — you must manually approve each transaction.
5
Search for ZPH by mint address
In Jupiter's swap interface, click the token selector and paste the official ZPH mint address to find the correct token. Always use the mint address — never search by name alone, as scam tokens can use the same ticker.
